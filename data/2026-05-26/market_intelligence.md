### Money & Macro：「Why Japan isn’t broke yet」
連結：https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hLwSSGM6tzc
時長：約 21 分鐘

**字幕內容：**

We all know that Japan has the highest government debt in the world, at 226% of GDP in 2023. That that is almost double that of France or the UK. Two countries that have faced repeated debt crisis, where investors are rapidly demanded higher interest rates in response to government spending plans. And yet, while Japan's economy has stagnated much more than these others do, and the population is aging much faster. Investors seem willing to keep lending Japan a lot of money at much lower interest rates than other countries. So what the heck is going on? Well, according to a recent paper by economists Tim Dou and Lustig, the answer may be surprisingly simple. Actually, everyone has just been looking at the wrong number. Yes, Japan's total official government debt is well over 220% of GDP, but that number leaves out something important. Unlike most Western governments, Japan's consolidated government, which includes institutions like the central Bank and public pension funds, holds a massive financial portfolio of stocks and bonds that was worth about 192% of GDP in 2020 fourth. Of course, total debt of the entire Japanese government and public pension funds and central banks was also higher than the official number at 270% of GDP.
